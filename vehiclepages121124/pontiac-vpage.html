OEM Parts and Accessories for Your Pontiac

Although Pontiac ceased production in 2010, it gained a loyal following in the American muscle car scene. First introduced in the 1960s, Pontiac arrived with innovative styling, V8 engine options, and a Wide-Track design for improved handling.

Let's explore three of our favorite Pontiac models:

Pontiac Aztek

The Aztek is a Pontiac early crossover SUV. Although widely panned for a polarizing design, the Aztek features a configurable rear cargo area and an optional tent kit, making it a hit with campers. If you're an adventurous driver, the Aztek might be the perfect choice.

Pontiac Solstice

The Solstice is a two-seat roadster well-known for its classic design cues and rear-wheel-drive layout. Plus, it was known for its affordability, making it a hit with the younger demographic.

Pontiac Bonneville

First launched in 1957, the Bonneville is a Pontiac full-size sedan offering a spacious cabin, a popular Wide-Track design, ample seating space, and several powerful engines. Today, it invokes nostalgia for its famous split grill and newfound collectible status among hardcore auto enthusiasts.

Common Pontiac Maintenance Issues

The Aztec, Solstice, and Bonneville have had their share of common maintenance issues, including but not limited to the following:

  • Coolant leaks
  • Power window regulator failures
  • HVAC system problems
  • Transmission issues
  • Faulty ABS
